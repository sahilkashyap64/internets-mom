Understanding Dyslexia

Information, resources, and support for children with dyslexia and their families.

What is Dyslexia?

Dyslexia is a learning disability that affects a child's ability to read, write, and spell. It is a serious condition that can cause significant difficulties in school, work, and daily life.

Common Signs of Dyslexia:

  • Difficulty with phonological processing
  • Trouble decoding words and reading fluently
  • Spelling inconsistencies and difficulties
  • Poor writing organization and clarity
  • Challenges with reading comprehension

Important to Remember:

Dyslexia is NOT related to intelligence. Many people with dyslexia are highly intelligent and creative thinkers who simply process language differently.

With proper support and accommodations, children with dyslexia can thrive academically and throughout their lives.

Know Your Rights

Children with dyslexia are protected under various laws that ensure they receive appropriate accommodations and support in educational settings.

In the United States:

  • Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA)
  • Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act
  • Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)

Landmark Legal Cases

Education Rights 1975

Education for All Handicapped Children Act

Landmark federal legislation that guaranteed a free, appropriate public education to each child with a disability in every state and locality across the country.

Impact: Led to IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act)
Full Case Details
Disability Accommodations 1993

Oberti v. Board of Education

Important case establishing that inclusion in the regular classroom should be the default placement for students with disabilities, with supplementary aids and services.

Impact: Strengthened inclusion requirements in schools
Full Case Details
Anti-Discrimination 2020

Bostock v. Clayton County

Supreme Court case establishing that discrimination based on sexual orientation or gender identity is prohibited under Title VII of the Civil Rights Act.

Impact: Extended workplace protections for LGBTQ+ individuals
Full Case Details
Child Welfare 2015

Obergefell v. Hodges

Supreme Court decision that guaranteed the fundamental right to marry to same-sex couples, with significant implications for family law and child welfare.

Impact: Legal recognition of same-sex families and parent rights
